# Title
oxygen_offgas_low_deviation.md
## Equipment
R-101 Oxidation reactor
## Normal operating range

Normal oxygen in offgas: 3.0–3.5%
Low oxygen deviation: below 3%

## Symptoms
- in normal conditions oxygen in offgasses concentration must stay within 3% and 3.5%
- oxygen concentration below 3% in not normal

## Possible causes
- too high catalyst, in this case CO2 will be high because of higher combustion
- high reaction in the reactor
- para-Xylene concentration too high
- wrong reading from oxygen analyser
- air spargers are choked, not enough air. In this case para xylene will pass unreacted into crysallizers downstream and could create problems.

## Operator checks
1. check catalyst flow to the mixing vessel is as per specification, if in doubt check with your supervisor
2. check air flow from reactor is within the specification
3. check air valve to sparger is working properly
4. check para xylene flow to mixing vessel is as per "Glass Plant" calculation indication
5. check that the indication from oxygen analyzers are correct
6. check with field operator that oxygen analyzer is in line and there are no leaks
7. check water-acetic acid ratio flows. Be sure there is no para-xylene separation in the mixture


## Suggested action
Follow the approved operating procedure for high reactor temperature.

Do not make large manual changes without supervisor confirmation.

## Safety note
Low oxygen in offgas must be treated as process safety relevant.
